About Cecilia

Artist Bio.

I am an artist born and raised in Zimbabwe, now living in Southern Sweden.
As a painter but foremost as a mother, I explore the layered experiences of selfhood, motherhood, daughterhood, and matrescence through my large-scale acrylic works.

My art seeks to enrich the dialogue of a new female revolution, one that anchors itself in the spiritual DNA connecting grandmothers, mothers, and daughters across time — past, present, and future.
Through my paintings, I trace the invisible threads that bind us, exploring womanhood as both memory and prophecy.

My Approach

My paintings are a sacred act - one of both resistance and restoration.
They arise from my response to the subtle, yet profound harm I witness in how social media shapes the inner lives of our daughters.

In a world defined by curated images and fleeting digital impressions, I turn to painting as a way to slow down time, recenter presence, and reclaim untold stories.

Through my art, I construct symbolic filters, not the digital kind, but spiritual veils that both reveal and protect.
These filters offer a deeper seeing: a layered truth shaped by love, ancestral memory, intuition, and the unseen forces that guide us.

My work is a call for change — a gentle yet unyielding demand for a new way of being.
In this fragmented world, our daughters grow up surrounded by false mirrors.
It is time for truth-telling, for the reclamation of the feminine through love.
