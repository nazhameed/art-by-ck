Why I paint

Reclaiming a future where our daughters see themselves whole.

I was raised amid the bougainvillea bloom of Edenfield in Bulawayo, Zimbabwe. Those colours echo through my studio in Southern Sweden where I paint to protect slow time, deep presence, and the sacred work of mothers guiding the next generation.

When the world feels loud with distortion, I return to the canvas. Layer by layer, I build spiritual filters that both reveal and protect the feminine stories gifted to us by our ancestors.

Edenfield is not a place of exile. It is a field of colour where women remain the constant centre, where light ripples across pigment and invites you to linger until dusk.
